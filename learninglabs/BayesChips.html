Why the machine is mathematically faithful

Because the world contains exactly 100 constructed cases, every probability is an exact fraction of counts. The “weight” for the hidden condition is literally the number of selected gold chips divided by 100, and the final update is literally the fraction of selected chips that are gold. This makes the physical story and the algebra identical.
Short Game Loop
Mastery Notes
1) Move the first dial and watch how a small base rate can keep the updated probability low even when the evidence looks impressive. 2) Hold the gold tray fixed and raise the evidence among steel chips; the update will weaken because the observation is less distinctive. 3) Flip from conditioning on E to conditioning on not E and notice that “absence of evidence” is also a valid update. 4) Try to predict the posterior from the counts alone before opening the equation ledger.
Tip: once you are comfortable, ignore the decimals and reason entirely with counts. For example, if you condition on E, the updated probability is simply: selected gold chips / all selected chips. The full Bayes equation reduces to that exact ratio in this 100-case world.
